History & Heritage Key Features

Heritage &
Evolution.

K-pop's origins date back to 1885 with changga, Western folk songs with Korean lyrics. Its modern era began in 1992 with Seo Taiji and Boys, who revolutionized the industry by integrating American hip-hop and R&B.

The late 1990s saw the birth of the first idol groups like H.O.T., established by pioneering agencies like SM, YG, and JYP. This era defined the "idol formula" of synchronized dancing and high-end production.
